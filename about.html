Early life of Estée Lauder

1908-2004

Estée Lauder was born Josephine Esther Mentzer. She came from a modest background—her father was a Hungarian Jewish immigrant, and her mother was born in the United States to immigrant parents. Lauder's interest in beauty products started early. Her uncle, a chemist, introduced her to the world of skincare, which led to her experimenting with homemade skin creams in her kitchen.

Founding of Estée Lauder Companies

1946

In 1946, Estée Lauder and her husband, Joseph Lauder, founded Estée Lauder Inc. with a focus on high-quality skincare, makeup, and fragrance products. One of the company’s first offerings was a face cream that Estée had developed herself. She began by selling her products directly to beauty salons and spas, offering personalized demonstrations to customers. Instead of relying on traditional advertising, Estée Lauder was a pioneer in personal selling, introducing the concept of free samples, which helped build a loyal customer base.

Growth and Innovation

1950s-1960s

Estée Lauder began expanding into department stores. In 1953, the company launched its first fragrance, Youth Dew, which became a game-changer. It was marketed as a bath oil but was also a perfume, and it quickly became a best-seller. Lauder was highly innovative in terms of marketing. She was one of the first to use television advertising and is credited with making the concept of "luxury beauty" accessible to a broader audience.

Expansion and International Reach

1970s-1990s

By the 1970s, Estée Lauder had expanded internationally, opening stores in Europe and Asia. The company continued to grow, releasing a variety of skincare, makeup, and fragrance products. In the 1980s and 1990s, Estée Lauder expanded further by acquiring several other beauty brands, such as Clinique (1968), M.A.C. Cosmetics (1994), Aveda (1997), and Bobbi Brown (1995), among others. These acquisitions helped cement the company’s position as a global leader in the beauty industry.

Continued Success and Innovation

1990s-Present

The 1990s and 2000s saw Estée Lauder continue to innovate, particularly with the rise of digital marketing and social media. The company embraced these platforms early on, using influencers and celebrity collaborations to promote its products. In recent years, Estée Lauder has also been focusing on sustainability and inclusivity, expanding its product ranges to cater to all skin tones and promoting eco-friendly initiatives.
